Born in Zamość, Poland. She studied Operngesang in Hochschule für Musik und Theater in Hamburg with prof. Ingrid Kremling-Domański, and with prof. Henryka Januszewska-Stańczyk and prof. Ewa Biegas at the Karol Szymanowski Music Academy in Katowice where she obtained her Ph.D. degree and has been working as an assistant professor since 2009.

Mrs. Brzezińska has participated with success in some vocal competitions, e.g. in Vrable 2007 (Slovakia), Trnava 2008 (Czech Republic), Warsaw 2007 (Poland), Vienna 2009 (Austria) or Ada Sari’s International Vocal Competition in Nowy Sącz 2011 (Poland).

In 2009 the soprano made her professional opera debut at The Kraków Opera (Poland) as Phillippe in Penderecki’s Teufel von Loudun. In 2010 she took part in the International Bach Festival in Świdnica (Poland) performing under the baton of Andrew Parrott. Since 2011 she is a soloist at The Wroclaw Opera (Poland) where she has performed e.g. in Mozart’s Zauberflöte (Königin der Nacht), or his Don Giovanni as Donna Anna in Mariusz Treliński’s visionary production under the baton of Lebanese-Polish conductor Bassem Akiki. The soprano has in the repertoire some of Verdi’s operas: Rigoletto (Gilda), Falstaff (Nanetta) or Un Ballo in Maschera (Oscar). She also sings the roles of Olympia in Offenbach’s Les Contes d’Hoffmann, Adele in J. Strauss’ Die Fledermaus, Frasquita in Bizet’s Carmen or Falke in R. Strauss’ Die Frau ohne Schatten. In March 2014 Mrs. Brzezińska gained critical acclaim performing the title role in Lucia di Lammermoor in the premiere performance of Donizetti’s masterpiece. The same year she appeared in Germany as Gilda in Verdi’s Rigoletto during Thurn und Taxis Schlossfestspiele Regensburg.

Mrs. Brzezińska has also a vast concert repertoire, e.g.: Exsultate jubilate (W. A. Mozart), Stabat Mater (G. B. Pergolesi), Judas Maccabäus (J. F. Haendel), Messe G-dur (F. Schubert), J. S. Bach’s cantats, Te Deum (M. A. Charpentier), Słopiewnie, Songs of a Fairy-tale Princess and Songs of an Infatuated Muezzin (K. Szymanowski), Mädchenblumen (R. Strauss), From Jewish Folk Poetry (D. Shostakovich). She also successfully performs songs of S. Rachmaninov, H. Wolf, G. Mahler, R. Strauss, F. Poulenc or F. Schubert. fot. Małgorzata Chrastek

Soprano Ewa Vesin commands a distinguished position among the Polish foremost classical singers. After her successful debut in ‘Die Walküre’, the Wagner Society announced:

“It was well worth the journey to Wrocław merely to hear and see a superlative performance of Sieglinde by Ewa Vesin, whose warm, bright voice and superb acting conveyed every nuance of the role. She was consummate in communicating all aspects of her part, including the pathos, indeed the monstrousness of her marriage, the hope of better future with the unknown fugitive, her despair at his death, and her joy when she learns she is carrying his child. Ewa Vesin not only looked every inch the part, but used her voice to such good effect that one was not obliged to make allowances for size, age, or lack of breath control. An altogether outstanding performance.”
Wagner News 2007

Ewa Vesin was a resident performer of the Wrocław Opera (2003-2009). It was at the Wrocław Opera that she made her debut as Sieglinde. Her previous engagements include leading operatic roles: Desdemona in ‘Otello’, Tosca, Giulietta in ‘Les Contes d’Hoffman’, Halka, Sieglinde in ‘Die Walküre’, Gutrune in ‘Götterdämmerung’, Wellgunde in ‘Das Rheingold’, Flora in ‘Chopin’, Leonora in ‘Il Trovatore’ among others.

She has performed under illustrious conductors such as Krzysztof Penderecki, Jacek Kaspszyk, Valery Gergiev, Paul McCreesh, Benjamin Shwartz, Alexander Liebreich, Eraldo Salmieri, Łukasz Borowicz, Tadeusz Strugała, Marek Pijarowski, Michał Klauza, Tadeusz Kozłowski, Wojciech Rodek, Tadeusz Serafin, Tomasz Tokarczyk.

In addition to her operatic engagements Ewa Vesin has performed in recitals toured Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Ukraine, Luxemburg, Czech Republic and China with renowned chamber musicians and philharmonic orchestras including: National Philharmonic in Warsaw, Polish National Radio Symphony Orchestra in Katowice, the Polis Radio Orchestra in Warsaw, with the Beijing National Philharmonic Orchestra she has appeared in Krzysztof Penderecki’s Symphony No8 under the composer’s baton.

Ewa Vesin appeared at a number of Festivals including Viva il Canto (Poland), Wratislavia Cantans (Poland), International Wagner Festival (Poland), Musica Polonica Nova (Poland), International Festival in Brno (Czech Republic), Singing Europe (Poland), Oper Air Festival in Xanten (Germany), Open Air Festival in Luxemburg, International Beijing Festival (China).

Her repertoire includes the symphonic masterpieces: Messa da Requiem G. Verdi, Mahler’s Symphony No2, Mahler’s Symphony No8, H. M. Górecki Symphony No3, Stabat Mater K. Szymanowski, L. van Beethoven Symphony No9, Credo K. Penderecki, Symphony No8 K. Penderecki.

Ewa Vesin received her Master of Music degree in Vocal Performance from the Karol Lipiński University of Music in Wrocław (Poland). In addition, she has studied under Doris Yarick-Cross and Thimothy Shaindlin at Yale University in New Haven.

Ewa Vesin has received several awards and honours: the Special Award and the Scholarship of the Minister of Culture and National Heritage, Gloria Artis Bronze Medal, and a nomination for the Golden Mask Theatre Award of the Silesian Voivodeship in the Vocal Acting Role category for her title role in Tosca by G. Puccini, directed by Tadeusz Bradecki at the Silesian Opera (Poland).

Born in Ivano-Frankovsk (Ukraine) in 1983, she began to play the violin at the age of 7 and finished D. Sichynsky Music College in 2002. In 2007 Mrs. Zhytynska graduated from the National Tchaikovsky Academy of Music in Kiev (Ukraine) under prof. N. Kurin and post-graduated training course under O. Dyachenko (vocal department). Her actual vocal teacher is Jolanta Zmurko.

In 2006 Mrs. Zhytynska made her professional debut in the Ukrainian National Opera in Kiev as Olga in Tchaikovsky’s Eugene Onegin. In December 2007 she appeared in the Wroclaw Opera (Poland) as Maddalena in Rigoletto and currently is a soloist of this opera house where she performs some Verdi and Mozart roles (Fenena in Nabucco, Emilia in Otello and Cherubino in Le Nozze di Figaro, Zerlina in Don Giovanni, Dorabella in Così fan tutte, Papagena in Zauberflöte). She gained critical acclaim singing title roles in premiere productions of Saint-Saëns’ Samson et Dalila and Bizet’s Carmen. Mrs. Zhytynska performes in Wrocław also in Borodin’s Prince Igor (Konchakovna) and Mussorgsky’s Boris Godunov (Marina Mnishek). She has also in her repertoire e.g. Siebel in Gounod’s Faust, Lubasha in Rimsky-Korsakov’s The Tzar’s Bride or Witch in Purcell’s Dido and Aeneas, and performs oratorios and songs, too.

The mezzosoprano appears often in other theatres as a guest artist: in December 2008 she debuted in Teatro Real in Madrid as Zefka in Janáček’s Zápisnik zmizelého (which she also performed Teatro la Maestranza in Sevilla and Teatro Arriaga in Bilbao). In October 2009 she debuted in Teatr Wielki – Opera Narodowa (TWON) in Warsaw as Hostess in Boris Godunov, in April 2010 in the Poznan Opera as Charlotte in Massenet’s Werther, in April 2011 in Teatro ABAO-OLBE (Bilbao) as Olga, and in May 2013 in Teatro Reggio (Torino) as Olga, too. In October 2013 Mrs. Zhytynska sang Maddalena with Leo Nucci in Teatro ABAO-OLBE, the same December she appeared in Tchaikovsky’s Iolanta in Warsaw, which was the coproduction with the Met Opera. In May 2014 she sang Princess Clarice in the premiere performance of Prokofiev’s The Love for Three Oranges in the Kraków Opera. The artist collaborated with many top conductors – Valery Gergiev, Gianandrea Noseda, Jari Hämäläinen, Miguel Ángel Gómez Martínez, Chikara Imamura, Keri-Lynn Wilson, Roberto Tolomelli, Łukasz Borowicz, Gabriel Chmura, Evgeny Volynsky or Bassem Akiki, and stage directors – Mariusz Treliński, Michał Znaniecki, Ignacio Garcia and Kasper Holten.

In June 2015 the mezzosoprano sang in a special concert with Roberta Alagna who invited her to perform under the baton of Giorgio Croci in Paris Philharmonic.

Future plans include Mrs. Zhytynska's appearance as Octavian in Strauss’ Rosenkavalier and Suzuki in Puccini's Madame Butterfly.

Born in 1985 in Kutno, Poland. He graduated with distinction from The Music Academy in Łódź with prof. Krzysztof Bednarek and prof. Piotr Miciński. He participated in masterclasses of e.g. Olga Pasiechnyk and Ryszard Karczykowski, as well as in some vocal competitions in Poland and all over the Europe.

Mr. Zuchowicz is a soloist at the Wrocław Opera (Poland) since 2009. He performs many principal roles like Nemorino in Donizetti’s L’elisir d’amore, Tamino in Mozart’s Die Zauberflöte or Stefan in Moniuszko’s Straszny dwór. The tenor sings with the company in some Verdi’s operas: Otello (Casio, Rodrigo), Giovanna d’Arco (Delil) or Falstaff (Bardolfo). He also appears as Normano in Donizetti’s Lucia di Lammermoor, Schuisky, Fiodor and Misail in Mussorgsky’s Boris Godunov, in R. Strauss’ Die Frau ohne Schatten or Offenbach’s Les Contes d’Hoffmann (Franz, Spalanzani). The artist took part in some of the Wrocław Opera’s megaproductions – Puccini’s Turandot (Pang), Borodin’s Prince Igor (Owlur) or Verdi’s Makbet (Malkolm).

Mr. Zuchowicz appears often as a guest soloist in many Polish philharmonics (Łódź or Rzeszów) and opera houses (e.g. Teatr Wielki in Łódź), and also abroad: in the Netherlands and Belgium with Opera Nova, or Luxembourg (Festival de Wiltz) and Germany (Sommerfestspiele Xanten). He collaborated with many top conductors: Łukasz Borowicz, Bassem Akiki, Dirca Vermeulena or Tadeusz Kozłowski.

The artist performs also song cycles of Schumann, Schubert, Chopin, Rachmaninov, Paderewski, Karłowicz and Moniuszko, as well as old Italian arias and Neapolitan songs. He loves singing operetta, too.

Mariusz Godlewski graduated prof. Bogdan Makal's voice class at the Karol Lipiński Academy of Music in Wrocław. In the years 2001-2002 he studied with Leopold Spitzer on a scholarship in Vienna.

The artist has won prizes at numerous competitions in Poland and abroad: Franciszka Platówna National Voice Competition in Wrocław (1996), Intercollegiate Voice Competition in Duszniki Zdrój (2000 – 1st place), a distinction at the 6th Adam Didur Opera Singing Competition in Bytom (2000), 1st Prize in the Imrich Godin International Vocal Competition (1999, Slovakia) and a distinction at the Hariclea Darclee International Voice Competition (2003, Romania).

Mr. Godlewski made his sensational debut on the stage of Teatr Wielki – Polish National Opera in the role of Pelleas in Debussy’s ‘Pelleas et Melisande’ (2002). Currently he collaborates with opera houses both in Poland and abroad.

In January 2011 in Polish National Philharmonic the artist performed the cycle of songs ‘Powiało na mnie morze snów... Pieśni zadumy i nostalgii’ (‘The Sea of Dreams Rushed Over Me – Songs of Reflection and Nostalgia’) by Krzysztof Penderecki during the world premiere of this piece under Valery Gergev who invited Mr. Godlewski to the White Nights Festival in St. Petersburg. In 2013 he sang during Beijing Music Festival under the baton of Maestro Penderecki. In 2014 he was named the best singer receiving Jan Kiepura’s Theatrical Music Award.

Mr. Godlewski performs many opera roles, as well as art song repertoire by Schubert and Brahms, or German and English Baroque music which were recorded for radio and television.
